### Past Research

**Type I Diabetes/Adoptive Cell Therapies**

In my first research experience I explored cellular engineering and how this can be used as a treatment to reorient the body to better fight a disease. In the context of this lab, we explored how re-engineering a specific type of immune cell called a regulatory T cell could hold off the onset of Type 1 Diabetes in mice. Not only could this research go on to the clinical setting one day, it is helping advance our understanding of tolerance in the immune system and autoimmune disease as a whole. 

**Citrus Greening Disease**

In this lab, I focused on a disease called Citrus Greening Disease. This bacteria-caused disease decimated Florida’s orange groves causing economic problems and permenantly damaging the infrastructure for growing oranges in the state. We focused on creating cultures of cells of the Asian Citrus Psylid which carries this bacteria from tree-to-tree with the hopes of using these cell lines to study and prevent this devestating plant disease.

**Neurodegenerative Disease**

This was my longest research experience spanning 2 years. Here we studied neurodegeneration through two models: 1) strokes 2) myotonic dystrophy. Our main interest was in the normal processes of regeneration in the brain as well as a certain cell type called neural stem cells or NSCs. I will not talk too much about this research here because it will likely be the topic of a few blog posts so please if you are interested go check them out there!

### Side Projects

**Streamlit**

I have been thoroughly enjoying exploring the streamlit web design framework. This is a library that allows for the easy creation of websites from python scripts and is very easy to work with while also being visually organized and appealing. This has been a fun side project to mess around with and I believe that when combined with plotly, this is a very promising way to view data and possibly communicate research in a public-friendly way. 

**The Blog**

This has become a large side project for me all alone. I have really loved getting to explore scientific communication and start trying to help people interested in the same field as me. Additionally, I have already started working on some posts in areas that I simply find interesting which has started making me look at what I am learning differently and giving me a greater appreciation for the complexity of cell biology.
