Well my first exam is officially behind me as I received my score today. This happened just in time for me to begin studying for my next one which is now in under 2 weeks. Let’s see what was done well and what needs to be changed!

### Style of Questions

I noticed that the style of questions asked was very much as I was anticipating. In very different form from my undergraduate, these questions asked me to create experiments, form hypotheses, and leverage my knowledge about the material to investigate the world. For example, in one question I was asked what I thought could be causing an outbreak of cancer in a zoo and then was asked how I could test my various hypotheses to find the cause. 

In another question, I was given a lollypop diagram of a gene and asked to theorize about its function, the role of mutations, and its relation to cancer based on background knowledge and the types/locations of frequent mutations. Of course, I was then asked what experiment could be designed in order to test this hypothesis. 

I really enjoyed answering this style of question for a few main reasons. Firstly, I have never been the kind of person who enjoys memorization. I often struggled to just memorize names, functions, and pathways. I much preferred to approach problems my own way leveraging my toolkit and focusing more on the application of my knowledge rather than my breadth of (not-so) fun facts. This type of questioning also much more realistically imitates what I need to be learning from my PhD. I am researcher now and my job is to hypothesize about the world and then test my hypotheses. 

I think that reading my scientific papers was a good way to study for questions like this because not only does doing that give me a good background understanding of the material but at the same time, I get a deeper conceptual understanding of how scientists are able to learn these things about cell biology. This allows me to both understand the pathways/materials/processes and to expand my toolbox of experiments. 

### Style of Responses

One thing that I wanted to address about this exam was the way that I answered my questions. I focused really hard while taking the exam and studying for it to be concise in my answers. This paid off for the most part; however, there was a question where I danced around using the scientific term for a process and as a result I lost a point. This was a very needless mistake and was at the end of the day because I was not concise enough in my answers. I think I did a good overall job with my concision but it is important for me to remember to not say more than is needed and to use the appropriate scientific jargon when I am able. 

### Studying

The biggest thing I can say about studying is that I want to more highly incorporate reading papers. This is something that I did towards the end of studying for my first exam because that is when I received the advice but I wish I would have started doing this earlier. I think that reading scientific papers was the most helpful thing that I did while studying for this exam and I am eager to incorporate this into my studying. 

The main difference that concerns me from the first exam to the second is that my second has a lot more pathways than the first. Our second exam covers the cell cycle, cell signaling, and cell development. This means that while reading papers will be a crucial part of my studying, I also want to put some targeted work into understanding the pathways and the key proteins in each pathway as the setting for my exam material. 

The way I want to do this is to ask myself the kinds of questions I anticipate being asked. For example, when looking at a pathway, “what proteins may be proto-oncogenes?”, “what proteins are tumor suppressors?”, “how would certain types of mutations affect the pathway and the cell as a whole?”. By asking myself these questions and deciding how I could test these experimentally, I think I will have a good understanding of our material and hopefully good practice for our exam. 

I am very happy with my performance on the first exam but I certainly have a lot of work left to do for this class and I will be doing all I can in the next two weeks to be just as proud of my second exam.
