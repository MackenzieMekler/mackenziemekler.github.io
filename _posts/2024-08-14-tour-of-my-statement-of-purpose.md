### What is a Statement of Purpose?

When I approached writing my statement of purpose for Ph.D. programs for the first time, my only experience was writing personal statements in high school. So that is what I wrote and when I got my first revision back it was bad enough that my mentor did not even mark up the paper, just told me to start again. Personal statements are the focus of undergraduate applications; however, for Ph.D. programs, a very similar sounding (but quite different) statement of purpose steals the spotlight. While a personal statement is more focused on the applicant, a statement of purpose is highly focused on the goals of an applicant and how experiences in the past and future shape these goals (to their credit the essays are very aptly named). 

While my original personal statement was filled with anecdotes about my time as an undergraduate, my statement of purpose quickly became focused and split into 3 main sections: 

1. Why do I want a Ph.D.? 
2. What have I done to prepare myself for a Ph.D.?
3. Why do I want a Ph.D. at your school?

This template became my statement of purpose and allowed me to make small changes to submit a very similar essay to all of my schools. Throughout this post, we will explore the evolution of my statement of purpose commenting on my feedback and exploring what creates a good essay. 

### Structure of This Post

For clarity, I decided to include this little section. I wanted to structure this post around the important questions that outlined my statement of purpose (SOP) and how the way I answered them evolved over iterations of feedback. In order to do this, I have made sections and shown excerpts from my various versions and discussed feedback I received, changes I made, and how or why I went about making changes. For each excerpt, I have included the paragraph that it appears in, this is to give a little context but is not intended to track all the way through, it is only important to consider that I am continuously answering the same question in each section. 

It is possible that later I will do a similar post where I review my final statement of purpose all the way through but that was not the purpose of this post. 

### Why do I want a Ph.D.?

For me, this is the section that I opened with but it is important to note that these questions need to be answered but not necessarily in the same order that I answered them. In fact, this section ended up as the beginning of my SOP but throughout my versions moved between the beginning, middle, and end. 

**Version 2.0** 

*Introductory paragraph*

> It took me 45 minutes to read the abstract of, and introduction to, my first research paper.  “Avidity and Bystander Suppressive Capacity of Human Regulatory T Cells Expressing De Novo Autoreactive T-Cell Receptors in T1D”. I was a first-year undergraduate entering the Brusko lab at the University of Florida. Each sentence introduced new terms that I needed to look up and each Google search led me down a rabbit hole of more research. This, my first introduction into the world of scientific research, fascinated me. A few weeks and 20 research articles later, I had learned more than I ever thought I could about Diabetes Mellitus. My passion for research had been ignited.
> 

**Version 2.0 Comments** 

The first comment that I received on this intro was that it was confusing. I start in the middle of a story without first explaining what brought me to my first lab. Additionally, while I begin to slightly hint at my interest in research, I do not explain why, just that I discovered it at a lab that I worked in.  In sum, my first attempt at answering this question comes off as convoluted and lacks concision. 

This is partly because I did not keep my audience in mind when writing this. As a prospective Ph.D. student, you will be evaluated by researchers and professors and thus it is best to be scientific and straight to the point. Often excessively creative writing will lose the audience or fail to convey your abilities as a scientist. This is something that this first revision taught me quickly. 

**Version 3.0** 

*Second Paragraph and Second-to-Last Paragraph* 

> The biomedical sciences have fascinated me for as long as I can remember. Throughout my life I was intrigued by how simple things such as moving your arm or fighting a cold relied on such intricate and complex mechanisms. In high school, this interest ignited as I was first exposed to the world of immunology. Here I was able to witness firsthand how the body races to defend itself from pathogens as these pathogens adapt and change to evade our bodies. This   beautiful dance between adaptability, evolution, and modern medicine inspired me to study medicine and microbiology which is a path I followed throughout my undergraduate education.
> 

> As an aspiring professor with a passion for research, a Ph.D. is an integral next step for me. UF would provide an ideal environment….
> 

**Noted Revisions** 

In my third version, this was not my opening paragraph but my second one. As you can see it is already much more to the point in answering the above question. In my first sentence, I establish my passion for the sciences which is then tied to a personal reference in the next sentences. This additionally relates to experiences in high school and then experiences as an undergraduate. 

Additionally, I answered this question again and better with one sentence in my conclusion paragraph as shown above. This sentence is directly to the point and highlights that I understand something important: no one gets a Ph.D. for fun. This degree is difficult and only done if a career necessitates it, which for me it does. This provides a clear goal and path for me which concisely explains my desire to get a Ph.D. 

**Version 3.0 Comments**

The biggest revision related to this question that I received during this draft was that I waited too long to mention this career goal. As I said, this sentence is perfect. It is concise. It is directly answering the important question. And it gives context to the rest of my statement of purpose. Because of this, I was advised to put it in the beginning and relate my other paragraph to it. 

**Version 4.0 (Final Version)**

> The biomedical sciences have fascinated me for as long as I can remember. Throughout my life I was intrigued by how simple things such as moving your arm or fighting a cold relied on such intricate and complex mechanisms. In high school, this interest ignited as I was first exposed to the world of immunology. Here I was able to witness firsthand how the body races to defend itself from pathogens as these pathogens adapt and change to evade our bodies. This   beautiful dance between adaptability, evolution, and modern medicine inspired me to study medicine and microbiology, which is a path I followed throughout my undergraduate education. This education showed me how academia would provide both the opportunity to meaningfully contribute to research while sharing my passions with a new generation of scientists after me. It was this insight that guided me into pursuing a Ph.D. with the goal of becoming a professor and PI.
> 

**Noted Revisions** 

This paragraph did not receive any notable revisions and thus it is my last edition. Some changes that I made to ensure this was to move my need for a Ph.D. to the stop and arrive at it naturally. First starting at my love for science which drove me to pursue it in high school and then through college. This then opened me to the career of a professor/researcher which naturally led me to need this degree. By flowing in this way, I both give context to the answer of why I want a Ph.D. and I use this question to give context to the rest of my essay about what I have done to put myself on this path. 

### Summary for Q1

In the end, I think the most helpful piece of feedback that I got from this section of my statement of purpose was to be concise and to directly answer this question. This is what inspired the sentence: “It was this insight that guided me into pursuing a Ph.D. with the goal of becoming a professor and PI.” This sentence very clearly states my why is to become a professor/PI and the proceeding paragraph sets up the why for this career being my passion for the sciences and research. A secondary advice to this is to not be showy. In my first edition shown here, I come off as very boastful and that is not the intent but it makes the paragraph less believable and relatable. The best thing that you can be in your statement of purpose as a whole and especially in this section is genuine. 

### What have I done to prepare myself for a Ph.D.?

This is the longest section of a Statement of Purpose. It is here where you are trying to sell yourself by showing how you have developed as a scientist and how you know you will succeed as a Ph.D. student. This can be done in a few ways but should always have at least one mention of academic research. In my case, I was fortunate enough to be very involved in research and thus my entire section walked through my different projects but other things such as classes, lab courses, personal reading/research into a field, and personal projects can be included as long as they achieve this goal. 

The most important thing about this section is to have it flow as a story. This should be about your development as a scientist so each experience should lead into the next while showing how you have grown from it. It is also important to mention that this section should go in depth. Your paper will be read by scientists so it is alright to get technical and talk about the details of your research. In fact it is encouraged and by structuring it well you can convey a deep understanding of what you have done. 

In this section, I will present things a little differently. I will try to show the flow through my final version using the previous ones to highlight important changes rather than through time largely for concision and clarity. 

**First Experience** 

> As an undergraduate, my passion for the biomedical sciences inspired me to pursue a research position in the Brusko lab which studies type 1 diabetes. I began with simple roles such as autoclaving hazardous waste or glassware and washing a variety of equipment. While I initially did not get to contribute to research, I was able to see the hard work that goes into keeping a lab running day to day. I eventually moved up to maintaining cell cultures, freezing and thawing cells, and learning proper aseptic technique. After developing my basic skills, I was able to become involved with an active project.
> 

While talking about washing dishes and menial lab tasks may not seem important, it can be a worthy inclusion to an essay. This is where a lot of people start and there is no shame in that. Labs take a lot of work to run and its important to understand that. Additionally, it’s valuable to show that you are able to earn responsibilities rather than just be handed them. However this being said the actual research should take a larger portion of the word count. 

**Second Experience**

> In this project we aimed to increase the binding of our lab’s transduced T cell receptor (TCR). We hypothesized that knocking out the endogenous TCR would decrease off-site binding and increase binding of the new TCR. In order to test this, I aided in multiple stages of this experiment. In the isolation step of the experiment, I helped use a cell sorter to obtain a pure population of T regulatory cells. Additionally, I maintained cultures of these cells while also using lentivirus and the CRISPR/Cas-9 system to express the exogenous TCR and to knockout the endogenous one. By doing this we were able to compare groups with and without knockout to determine that the knockout of the endogenous TCR increased avidity by decreasing off-site binding.
> 

This paragraph talks about my first project as a researcher. The paragraph is structured very intentionally from hypothesis to testing (saying what you actually did included here) to results. By structuring the paragraph this way you not only stay concise but also communicate like a scientist.

**Fourth Experience** 

> My honors thesis project has a focus on the lab’s research in myotonic dystrophy. In this project, I am examining the role of muscleblind-like (MBNL) proteins in neural stem cells *in vitro* with various neural stem cell lines derived from mouse models with knockouts of MBNL proteins. My hypothesis is that loss of MBNL proteins will impair differentiation and proliferation of these cells. I am testing this hypothesis by growing these various cell lines into spheres and quantifying size of the neurospheres from brightfield images. To test the impact on differentiation, I am using immunofluorescence to label neuroblasts for further quantification. In addition to these studies, I am investigating cell lines from mouse models more specific to myotonic dystrophy type 1 and type 2 that have toxic repetitive RNA expression that more accurately models these diseases. Throughout this project, I have worked to plan experiments, troubleshoot problems, and collect and analyze data. This project has given me insight into what planning and starting experiments requires as well as some of the difficulties that arise when attempting new assays. While challenging, the work in this project fascinates me and being able to conduct research to answer questions that no one currently understands is something that I have found a passion for.
> 

This project is structured in a very similar way to the first real project I wrote about but is done much more in-depth as I was much more involved. Additionally, as an ongoing project, this was talked about slightly differently. However, this still started with goals and hypotheses and moved into testing and current results. This paragraph also ends with directly talking about how I have grown as a researcher from this experience. This is a clear and concise way to present my research experience while also showing how it has prepared me for my next steps in a Ph.D. program. 

### Why do I want a Ph.D. at your school?

This is the main section that needs to be changed from school to school. It is your chance to show off the research that you have done on whatever schools you are looking at. More than that it is your first chance to organize the reasons that you want to go to a school. If you find it very difficult to write this section you may need to question why the school you are applying to is on your list. 

**Version 2.0**

> Specifically, a Ph.D. at the University of Florida would give me the opportunity to study under incredible researchers and professors. Additionally, I would be able to study a myriad of fields including cancer diagnostics and cancer immunology.
> 

**Noted Revisions**

The biggest revision that I had on this part is that this was way too little information and very non-specific. At this point, I had not done enough research on my school and had not included enough to receive viable feedback yet. 

**Version 3.0** 

> The University of Florida’s biomedical sciences Ph.D. program would provide a perfect environment for me to pursue this passion.  My interest in cancer biology draws me to UF due to the vast array of cancer research at the university as well as the recently established National Cancer Institute designation. My research interests are primarily in oncology but I am also interested in the interactions between tumors and the immune system in the tumor microenvironment. The biomedical sciences program would allow me to concentrate in cancer biology to further study this passion. Additionally, as an undergraduate I have experienced the collaborative environment at UF which allows for incredible interdisciplinary research. These reasons assure me that UF’s graduate program in the biomedical sciences would be a good next step in my educational journey.
> 

**Noted Revisions** 

This was the largest revision that I made to this section. I did a lot of research on the school to find all of the reasons that I loved the program as well as connecting it to personal reasons that I would like the school (unique to UF because this is the school I attended as an undergraduate). 

### Summary of Q3

This is a section that I hesitate to give too much structure to. In the end, this will be very personalized from person to person and for each school that an application is written to. The important thing to mention here is the depth of information shown and the types of things mentioned. 

Be genuine about why you want to go to the school. Remember that if you over-embellish at this step you are mainly hurting yourself. If you do not know where to start, specific centers at a school are always worth mentioning as well as any accreditation that made the school stand out to you. The NCI-designated cancer center at UF is an example of both of these.  Another thing worth mentioning is programs. I am interested in teaching so any schools that had programs for their graduate students to get classroom time stood out to me and I was certain to mention that. Finally, if you have room it is a good idea to name drop professors with whom you would be interested in working. This demonstrates the depth of your research into the school and is especially good if you have established a connection with said professor.  

### Conclusion

This is a little tour through my statement of purpose. I hope that reading this (while it was a lot) was helpful. It is important to note that the choices that I made for my statement of purpose were right for me but this is not the only way to write one and writing it differently does not mean you have done it wrong. I wish the best of luck to all of you and hope your writing and applications go well!
